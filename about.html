Three Pillars of Sustainability

Though sustainability is most commonly linked to the environment, we must also consider the economical and the social aspects in society to promote sustainability. These three spheres, the economic, social, and environmental spheres, are recognized as the three pillars of sustainability.

Economic

Currently, the supply and demand market is

consumerist in nature. In comparison to

decades before, products have shorter

lifecycles and are made from cheaper

materials and labor. These factors,

combined with the ease of online shopping,

contributes to the increased amount of waste

Sustainability aims to break this cycle

while considering the job market and profit.

Social

The social pillar is the most difficult to

define, largely due to the variety of issues

it covers. The social pillar covers topics

such as sustainable housing, quality of

life, diversity, resource equitity, and

unions. To summarize, anything that involves

the general community or any form of

collaboration, resulting in a lot of overlap

with the other pillars.

Environmental

The general idea behind the environmental

pillar is that we need to try to conserve

more of our natural resources, such as trees,

and water, and rely less on products that

emit harmful substances to the environment.

This pillar covers a variety of issues such

as renewable energy, diet, and recycling.

This pillar is the most well known since

it covers the some of the most urgent issues.
